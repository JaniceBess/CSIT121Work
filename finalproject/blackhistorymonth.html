Black History Month

Black History Month began as Black History Week in 1926.

Carter G. Woodson and the Association for the Study of Negro Life and History (ASNLH) started this observation, which coincided with Abraham Lincoln's Birthday.

A Black History Month was first propsed by teachers and students at Kent State University 1969.
