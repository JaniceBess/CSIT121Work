Juneteenth

Even though the Emancipation Proclamation went into effect in 1863, it could not be implemented in places still under Confederate control.

In western Texas, which was a Confederate State,slaves would not be free until June 19, 1865, when Union troops went to Galveston Bay announcing that 250,000 slaves were free by executive decree.

The newly freed slaves named that day as "Juneteenth.""

Why I chose this Image

I chose this image again because it holds the colors of the African-American Flag: Red, black, and green, but also because it shows black wrists freed from the shackles of slavery.
