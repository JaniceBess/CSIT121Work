What is the Speech Recognition Grammar Specification?

Speech Recognition Grammar Specification (SRGS) consists of two syntaxes for defining grammars, Augmented Backus-Naur Form (ABNF) and Grammar Extensible Markup Language (GrXML).

  • Augmented Backus-Naur Form (ABNF)
  • Grammar Extensible Markup Language (GrXML)

Backus-Naur

Augmented Backus-Naur Form is basically an version of Backus-Naur Form is a notation system that defines the syntax of programming languages and other formal languages. It was developed by John Backus and Peter Naur and is a metasyntax for context-free grammars, providing a precise way to outline the rules of a language's structure.

John Backus & Peter Naur

John Backus, worked at IBM and John Backus developed Fortran (Formula Translator), one of the first general purpose, high-level computer programming languages. Peter Naur was a Danish astronomer and computer scientist. Both served on the international committees developing the ALGOL system of programming languages, which is where they developed the Backus-Naur Form, describing the structure of programming languages

Grammar XML

Grammar XML (GrXML) is a markup language used in speech recognition systems to describe the grammar of a spoken language, including the structure and vocabulary of the language, as well as the rules for recognizing spoken words and phrases.

Grammars

A grammar tells the speech recognizer what to listen for, and what to return to the application when speech is recognized. Like any XML document, a grammar file must begin with a header that specifies certain characteristics of the grammar. The main body of a grammar file consists of grammar rules that define the spoken words recognized by the grammar, and the corresponding variable values that will be returned for the recognized items.
