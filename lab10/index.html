Speech Recognition Grammar Specification

Speech Recognition Grammar Specification is a W3C (the World Wide Web Consortium)standard for how speech recognition grammars are specified. A speech recognition grammar is a set of word patterns, and tells a speech recognition system what to expect a human to say.

The primary use of a speech recognizer grammar is to permit a speech application to indicate to a recognizer what it should listen for, specifically words that may be spoken, patterns in which those words may occur, and spoken language of each word.
